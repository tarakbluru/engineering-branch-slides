# Engineering Branch Slide Deck - Content Outline

## Document Purpose
This document defines the detailed content structure for each slide type across all engineering branch presentations.

## Content Research & Sourcing Strategy

### Primary Sources
1. **Academic Institutions**:
   - MIT OpenCourseWare (ocw.mit.edu)
   - Stanford Engineering (engineering.stanford.edu)
   - IIT curriculum documents
   - AICTE model curriculum

2. **Professional Organizations**:
   - IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers)
   - ACM (Association for Computing Machinery)
   - Professional society publications

3. **Industry Sources**:
   - LinkedIn Salary Insights
   - Glassdoor career data
   - Stack Overflow Developer Survey
   - Tech company engineering blogs

4. **Educational Video Sources**:
   - MIT OpenCourseWare YouTube
   - Crash Course Engineering
   - 3Blue1Brown (for mathematical concepts)
   - Computerphile / Numberphile
   - Branch-specific educational channels

### Image Sources
- Unsplash.com (free, high-quality images)
- Pexels.com (free stock photos)
- Wikimedia Commons (educational images)
- Company websites (logos, with attribution)
- Educational institution websites (with attribution)

## Slide-by-Slide Content Template

### Slide 1: Title Slide

**Visual Layout**:
- Full-screen background image (relevant to branch)
- Centered text overlay with semi-transparent background
- Clean, modern typography

**Content Elements**:
```
[Branch Name in Full]
[Engaging Tagline - one sentence capturing essence]
[Background: Relevant hero image]
```

**Speaker Notes Template**:
```
Welcome to this comprehensive overview of [Branch Name]. 

[Opening hook - why this branch is exciting/relevant]

This presentation will take you through what you'll study, where you can work, 
and how this branch is evolving in the age of artificial intelligence. 

Whether you're considering this as your career path or just exploring your 
options, this guide will give you a complete picture of what [Branch Name] 
really involves.

[Duration: ~45 seconds]
```

**Example Images**:
- Circuit boards (EC)
- Code on screen (CSE)
- Power grid (EE)
- Data visualizations (Data Science)

## Branch-Specific Customization

Each of the 7 branches will follow this template but with customized content:

### 1. Computer Science & Engineering (CSE)
- Focus: Software, algorithms, systems
- Career emphasis: Tech companies, startups, product development
- AI Impact: High (both threat and opportunity)

### 2. Electronics & Communication (EC)
- Focus: Circuits, signals, communication systems
- Career emphasis: Telecom, embedded systems, IoT
- AI Impact: Moderate (automation of design, enhanced systems)

### 3. Electrical Engineering (EE)
- Focus: Power systems, control, energy
- Career emphasis: Power sector, automation, renewable energy
- AI Impact: Moderate (smart grids, predictive maintenance)

### 4. Information Science
- Focus: Data management, information systems, networks
- Career emphasis: IT services, system architecture, databases
- AI Impact: High (data engineering for AI, information retrieval)

### 5. CSE - Data Science
- Focus: Statistics, ML basics, analytics, big data
- Career emphasis: Data analyst, business intelligence, ML engineer
- AI Impact: Very High (core AI field)

### 6. CSE - AI & ML
- Focus: Deep learning, NLP, computer vision, AI systems
- Career emphasis: AI research, ML engineering, AI product development
- AI Impact: Extremely High (building AI itself)

### 7. CSE - Cyber Security
- Focus: Security principles, cryptography, ethical hacking
- Career emphasis: Security analyst, penetration testing, compliance
- AI Impact: Dual (AI tools for security + AI threats to defend against)

## Quality Assurance Checklist

Before finalizing content for each branch, verify:

- [ ] All technical information is accurate and current (2025-2026)
- [ ] Salary data is realistic and sourced
- [ ] Images have proper attribution
- [ ] Video links are functional and relevant
- [ ] Speaker notes are comprehensive (150-300 words per slide)
- [ ] Language is simple and accessible (no unexplained jargon)
- [ ] Balanced perspective (not overly promotional or negative)
- [ ] AI impact is realistic and actionable
- [ ] Sources are cited appropriately
- [ ] Content is appropriate for high school student audience

## Expected Outcome

Each completed slide deck will:
1. Provide comprehensive, accurate information (15-20 slides)
2. Engage through visuals and clear writing
3. Enable informed decision-making
4. Be AI-parseable for video generation
5. Take approximately 30-40 minutes to present fully (with speaker notes)
