## How to Use the Slide Decks

### Opening the Slides

1. Navigate to the `slides/` folder
2. Double-click `EC.html` (or any branch file) to open in your default browser
3. Alternatively, right-click and choose "Open with" → your preferred browser
4. **Best Experience:** Use Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge (latest versions)

### Navigation Controls

**Mouse/Touch:**
- Click "Next →" and "← Previous" buttons at bottom of screen
- Swipe left/right on touch devices (tablets, phones)

**Keyboard Shortcuts:**
- `→` or `↓` or `Space`: Next slide
- `←` or `↑`: Previous slide
- `Home`: Jump to first slide
- `End`: Jump to last slide
- `N`: Toggle speaker notes (shows/hides detailed narration)

**Progress Tracking:**
- Thin progress bar at top shows completion percentage
- "Slide X of 20" counter in top-right corner

### Speaker Notes for AI Video Generation

**Accessing Notes:**
- Press `N` key to toggle speaker notes visibility
- Each slide has 200-500+ words of detailed narration
- Includes context, explanations, examples, and delivery guidance

**Using with AI Video Tools (Gemini, etc.):**
1. Upload the HTML file to AI video generation tool
2. The tool reads both slide content and speaker notes
3. Generates narrated video presentation automatically
4. Speaker notes are comprehensive enough for quality narration

### Printing to PDF

1. Open the slide deck in browser
2. Press `Ctrl+P` (Windows) or `Cmd+P` (Mac)
3. Choose "Save as PDF" as the printer
4. Adjust settings:
   - Landscape orientation recommended
   - Include background graphics
   - Adjust margins if needed
5. Save the PDF

**Note:** Speaker notes will automatically appear in print/PDF version.

### Viewing on Mobile/Tablet

The slides are responsive and work well on tablets and phones:
- Swipe gestures for navigation
- Auto-scaling text and layouts
- Touch-friendly navigation buttons
- Optimized for screens 7" and larger
