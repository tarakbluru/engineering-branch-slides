## 1. Robotics & Automation Engineering

### Overview
Robotics & Automation Engineering is an interdisciplinary field combining mechanical, electrical, computer science, and control systems engineering. The curriculum emphasizes physics-based design, modeling, and control of robotic systems.

### Year-Wise Curriculum

#### **Year 1: Foundation Courses**

**Semester 1:**
- Mathematics I (Calculus and Linear Algebra)
- Physics I (Mechanics and Thermodynamics)
- Chemistry/Materials Science
- Engineering Graphics and Design
- Introduction to Programming (C/C++)
- Electronics Workshop

**Semester 2:**
- Mathematics II (Differential Equations)
- Physics II (Electromagnetism and Optics)
- Electrical Circuits and Analysis
- Digital Electronics
- Data Structures and Algorithms
- Engineering Mechanics Lab

#### **Year 2: Core Engineering**

**Semester 3:**
- Applied Thermodynamics
- Mechanics of Solids
- Signals and Systems
- Microprocessors and Microcontrollers
- Electrical Measurements and Instrumentation
- Digital Electronics Lab

**Semester 4:**
- Machine Design Fundamentals
- Control Systems I
- Embedded Systems
- Sensor Technology and Applications
- Programming in Python
- Embedded Systems Lab

#### **Year 3: Specialization**

**Semester 5:**
- Robotics Fundamentals (Kinematics and Dynamics)
- Industrial Automation and PLC Programming
- Computer Vision and Image Processing
- Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
- Actuators and Drive Systems
- Robotics Lab I

**Semester 6:**
- Advanced Control Systems
- Mobile Robotics and Navigation
- Robot Operating System (ROS 2)
- Motion Planning and Path Finding
- Human-Robot Interaction
- Robotics Lab II

#### **Year 4: Advanced Topics and Projects**

**Semester 7:**
- Autonomous Systems
- Multi-Robot Systems
- Industrial Robotics and Applications
- Elective I (Choose from specialization areas)
- Elective II (Choose from specialization areas)
- Mini Project

**Semester 8:**
- Major Project/Capstone Design
- Industrial Internship/Training
- Seminar and Presentation
- Professional Ethics and Safety

### Core Subject Areas

#### **Robotics Core:**
- Planar and Spatial Kinematics
- Multi-Rigid-Body Dynamics
- Mechanism Design
- Motion Planning Algorithms
- Robot Manipulator Control
- Actuator and Sensor Integration

#### **Automation Core:**
- Programmable Logic Controllers (PLC)
- SCADA Systems
- Industrial Communication Protocols

### Elective Options

**Specialization Electives:**
- Autonomous Vehicles and Self-Driving Technology
